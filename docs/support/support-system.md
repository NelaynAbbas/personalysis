# PersonalysisPro Support Ticketing System

This document outlines the support ticketing system for PersonalysisPro, including support channels, ticket management, and resolution procedures.

## Support Channels

PersonalysisPro offers multiple channels for users to request support:

1. **In-App Support Widget**: Available on all pages within the application
2. **Email Support**: support@personalysispro.com for general inquiries
3. **Phone Support**: Available for Enterprise clients during business hours
4. **Knowledge Base**: Self-service support articles and tutorials
5. **Community Forum**: Peer-to-peer support for common questions

## Ticket Creation

### Via In-App Widget

1. Click the "Help" button in the bottom-right corner of any screen
2. Select "Create Support Ticket"
3. Fill in the required information:
   - Issue category
   - Subject
   - Description
   - Priority level
   - Attachments (optional)
4. Submit the ticket

### Via Email

Send an email to support@personalysispro.com with:
- Clear subject line indicating the issue
- Detailed description of the problem
- Screenshots or attachments if applicable
- Your contact information and account details

### Via Phone (Enterprise Only)

1. Call the dedicated enterprise support line
2. Provide account verification information
3. Describe the issue to the support agent
4. A ticket will be created and tracked in the system

## Ticket Categories

All tickets are categorized for efficient routing and resolution:

1. **Account Issues**:
   - Login problems
   - Access permissions
   - Account verification
   - Billing inquiries

2. **Technical Issues**:
   - Application errors
   - Performance problems
   - Integration issues
   - Data import/export problems

3. **Feature Requests**:
   - Suggestions for new features
   - Enhancement requests
   - Integration possibilities

4. **Survey Management**:
   - Survey creation issues
   - Response collection problems
   - Data analysis challenges
   - Report generation errors

5. **Security Concerns**:
   - Data privacy questions
   - Security vulnerabilities
   - Compliance inquiries

## Ticket Priority Levels

Tickets are assigned priority levels that determine response and resolution timelines:

1. **Critical**: System-wide outage or security breach
   - Initial response: Within 30 minutes
   - Resolution target: 4 hours
   - 24/7 support

2. **High**: Significant functionality impaired for multiple users
   - Initial response: Within 2 hours
   - Resolution target: 8 hours
   - Extended business hours support

3. **Medium**: Limited functionality issues affecting specific features
   - Initial response: Within 8 hours
   - Resolution target: 24 hours
   - Business hours support

4. **Low**: Minor issues, cosmetic problems, or enhancement requests
   - Initial response: Within 24 hours
   - Resolution target: 72 hours
   - Business hours support

## Ticket Lifecycle

All tickets follow a standardized lifecycle:

1. **Created**: Ticket is submitted by user or support agent
2. **Acknowledged**: Support team has received and reviewed the ticket
3. **In Progress**: Agent is actively working on resolution
4. **Pending User Input**: Waiting for additional information from user
5. **Resolved**: Issue has been addressed or solution provided
6. **Closed**: User has confirmed resolution or ticket auto-closed after 7 days

## Escalation Process

If a ticket requires escalation:

1. **First-Level Escalation**:
   - Ticket is escalated to a senior support agent
   - User is notified of escalation
   - Response time is expedited

2. **Second-Level Escalation**:
   - Ticket is escalated to technical specialists or developers
   - User is updated on escalation status
   - Detailed technical analysis is performed

3. **Management Escalation**:
   - For critical issues or extended resolution timeframes
   - Support manager is assigned to oversee resolution
   - Regular updates are provided to the user

## Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

### Standard Plan

- Business hours support (9 AM - 5 PM, Monday-Friday)
- Email and in-app support only
- Initial response:
  - High priority: 4 hours
  - Medium priority: 12 hours
  - Low priority: 24 hours

### Professional Plan

- Extended business hours (8 AM - 8 PM, Monday-Friday)
- Email, in-app, and limited phone support
- Initial response:
  - Critical priority: 2 hours
  - High priority: 3 hours
  - Medium priority: 8 hours
  - Low priority: 16 hours

### Enterprise Plan

- 24/7 support for critical issues
- All support channels available
- Dedicated account manager
- Initial response:
  - Critical priority: 30 minutes
  - High priority: 1 hour
  - Medium priority: 4 hours
  - Low priority: 8 hours

## Monitoring and Reporting

The support team monitors and reports on service levels:

- Weekly reports on ticket volume and resolution rates
- Monthly SLA compliance reports
- Quarterly service quality reviews
- Annual customer satisfaction analysis

## Knowledge Base Integration

The support ticketing system is integrated with the knowledge base:

1. When submitting a ticket, users receive suggested articles based on their issue
2. Common ticket topics trigger creation of new knowledge base articles
3. Support agents can link tickets to relevant knowledge base content
4. Resolved tickets may be converted to knowledge base articles (with customer permission)

## Continuous Improvement

The support process undergoes continuous improvement:

1. Customer satisfaction surveys after ticket resolution
2. Regular review of common issues to improve product
3. Support team training based on emerging trends
4. Quarterly review of support processes and SLAs

- **Tier 2**: Technical specialists for complex problems
- **Tier 3**: Product developers and engineers for advanced troubleshooting
- **Account Managers**: Dedicated support for enterprise clients
